Why not the usual ways

  
What you actually get back

  
Same table on screen, four ways to grab it.

  

    

      
⌘⇧4 screenshot

      
A picture of the data. You can look at it. You can't paste it, sort it, or search it.

    

    

      
Live Text

      
The words, without the structure. Columns collapse into one line. Code loses its indentation.

    

    

      
Paste into a chatbot

      
An answer, eventually. Attach, explain, wait, copy back. Your screen is now in a datacenter.

    

    

      
Beaver

      
The data itself, on your clipboard, in the shape it had on screen. No upload, no chat, no meter.

    

  






  
If you feed an AI

  

    

      
Send your model text, not a photo of text.

      

        Models bill images by their pixels. A full-window screenshot costs
        around 2,000 tokens before the model says a word, and big
        images get downscaled before the model reads them. Small text turns
        into guesswork, and a guessed digit looks exactly like a real one.
      

      

        Beaver hands your model the same content as exact Markdown:
        a fraction of the tokens, nothing lost in the pixels.
        Better input, better answers, smaller bill.

Private by architecture

    
Captures have no network path.

    

      

        
On-device vision

        
The model runs locally: MLX on Apple Silicon, llama.cpp on Intel. A capture is processed and forgotten by everything except your own history file.

      

      

        
Open source, checkable

        
MIT licensed. Read the code, build it yourself, watch the network do nothing. The only network call is an optional update check, and one flag turns it off.

      

      

        
Safe for the sensitive stuff

        
Invoices, contracts, patient notes, anything under NDA. Extract the data without it ever leaving the room.
