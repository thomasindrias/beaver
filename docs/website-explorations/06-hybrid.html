thank you for reading the fine print

1You pay by the pixel

Models bill images by their dimensions. A window screenshot runs about 1,900 tokens before the model says a word.1 The table inside it, as Markdown, is usually under 200.

2The model reads a shrunk copy

Big screenshots get downscaled before the model sees them.2 The crisp 9-point numbers on your Retina display land below what the vision encoder can resolve.

3It guesses, confidently

When a model can't read a digit, it doesn't say so. It makes one up, in the same font as the real ones. Swapped columns and invented cells look exactly like data.

In one published evaluation, GPT-4V answered 35 of 50 table questions incorrectly.3

Beaver reads the exact region you box, at full resolution, on your Mac. Your model gets exact text at a tenth of the tokens. Better input, better answers, smaller bill.

Your data sleeps at home.

Beaver's vision model runs on your Mac. A capture is processed locally and lands on your clipboard; there is no server to send it to. Privacy here isn't a promise in a policy, it's how the thing is built. That matters for invoices, contracts, patient notes, and anything under NDA.

  • On-device model: MLX on Apple Silicon, llama.cpp on Intel
  • Open source (MIT), build it from source if you like
  • History is a local SQLite file, yours to keep or delete
  • Only network call: an optional update check. BEAVER_DISABLE_UPDATE_CHECK=1 kills it.
  • Works offline. Works on a plane. And you can verify all of this: read the source, or watch Little Snitch do nothing.
