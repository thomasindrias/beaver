An argument, with receipts

A screenshot is the most expensive way to send text to an AI.

It feels free: snap, paste, ask. But the model bills you for every pixel, reads a shrunk copy, and guesses at what it can't see. Beaver is a Mac utility that grabs the same content as exact Markdown instead, on-device, in one drag.

thank you for reading the fine print

1You pay by the pixel

Vision models bill images by their dimensions. On Claude, an image costs roughly its pixel count divided by 7501, so an ordinary 1440×900 window screenshot runs about 1,900 tokens before the model has said a single word. The table inside that screenshot, written out as Markdown, is usually under 200.

Do that a few dozen times a day, in a team, inside agent loops that re-read the conversation on every turn, and "just paste the screenshot" quietly becomes the most expensive line in the budget.

2The model reads a shrunk copy

Providers cap image sizes. Anything big gets downscaled before the model sees it2, and vision encoders read the result in coarse patches. Text that was crisp on your Retina display lands below the resolution the encoder can resolve. The 9-point numbers in your dashboard? The model is reading this:

3It guesses, confidently

Here's the part that should bother you. When a model can't resolve a digit, it doesn't say so. It produces the most plausible digit. A hallucinated 7 is rendered in the same font as a real one.

In one published evaluation, GPT-4V answered 35 of 50 table-reasoning questions incorrectly.3

Swapped columns, invented cells, merged rows. For a meme, fine. For an invoice you're about to book, a config you're about to deploy, or a statement you're reconciling, "plausible" is the failure mode.

4And you did all that in a chat window

Open the tab. Attach. Type "extract this table". Wait. Copy the answer. Fix the formatting. That's five steps and half a minute for something that should be a copy. Meanwhile your screenshot, which might be a customer's data or a contract under NDA, now lives in someone else's infrastructure.

5And some screens should never leave the room

The cost argument assumes you're allowed to upload the screenshot at all. Often you're not. Invoices, bank statements, patient notes, contracts under NDA, a customer's data in your admin panel. The moment any of it lands in a chat window, it sits in someone else's infrastructure, under someone else's retention policy, forever outside your control.

That's not a knock on any provider's ethics. It's just what uploading means. For those screens the question isn't "is a screenshot cheaper", it's "why is this leaving the building at all".

None of this means vision models are bad. It means an image is the wrong container for text you could have sent exactly, and the wrong trip for data that should have stayed home.

Send the text, keep the pixels.

Beaver lives in your menu bar. Press ⌘⇧D, drag a box around the table, the code, the slide. A vision model running on your Mac reads that exact crop at full resolution and puts clean Markdown on your clipboard. Paste it into Claude, a prompt, a pipeline, a spreadsheet.
