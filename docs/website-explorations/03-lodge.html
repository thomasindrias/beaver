"Can't I just paste it into ChatGPT?"

You can. It's a whole thing: open the chat, attach, explain what you want, wait, copy back, fix the formatting. And your screen just took a trip to a datacenter.

There's a quieter problem too. Models bill screenshots by pixels and shrink them before reading. Small text turns to guesswork, and a guessed digit looks exactly like a real one.

Your data sleeps at home.

Beaver's vision model runs on your Mac. A capture is processed locally and lands on your clipboard; there is no server to send it to. Privacy here isn't a promise in a policy, it's how the thing is built.

  • On-device model: MLX on Apple Silicon, llama.cpp on Intel
  • Open source (MIT), build it from source if you like
  • History is a local SQLite file, yours to keep or delete
  • Only network call: an optional update check. One flag kills it.
  • Works offline. Works on a plane. Works under NDA.
