§2

Life of a capture

From keystroke to clipboard, all local.

Shortcut pressed t+0

You hit ⌘⇧D. A capture overlay covers the screen. Any app, any video, any call.

Region cropped local

You drag a box around the thing you need. Only that crop moves forward, at full native resolution. Nothing is shrunk, nothing is uploaded.

Vision model reads it on-device

A local model (Qwen2.5-VL via MLX on Apple Silicon, MiniCPM-V via llama.cpp on Intel) extracts the content with structure intact: tables, lists, code, indentation.

Result lands t+seconds

Clean Markdown is on your clipboard and in a local SQLite history you can search, export, or delete. Done. No chat window ever opened.

The whole table. It's short.

extracted textneverclipboard + local SQLite only
capture historynevera file on your disk; yours to delete
telemetry / analyticsnevernone exists in the codebase
update checkoptionala version number against GitHub Releases, once a day; disable with one flag

Kill switch: BEAVER_DISABLE_UPDATE_CHECK=1 turns off the only network call there is.

§5

Why this matters

Some screens can't go to a chatbot.

Invoices. Bank statements. Patient notes. Contracts under NDA. Legal discovery. The moment a screenshot lands in a chat window, it's in a datacenter and subject to somebody else's retention policy. Beaver extracts the data while it stays in the room.

And it's better input for AI anyway.

When you do want a model's help, send it Beaver's output instead of the screenshot. Exact text at roughly a tenth of the tokens, with nothing lost to image downscaling. The model reasons about your data instead of squinting at a shrunk picture of it.

OCR-fast. Chatbot-smart. Neither one's compromise.

Live Text is instant but flattens tables into word soup. Cloud AI understands structure but is slow, metered, and elsewhere. Beaver is the missing middle: structure survives, and it never leaves.
